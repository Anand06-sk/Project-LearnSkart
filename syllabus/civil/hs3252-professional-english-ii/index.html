HS3252 PROFESSIONAL ENGLISH - II

L T P C: 2 0 0 2 | Total: 30 Periods

COURSE OBJECTIVES

  • To improve reading and writing skills through meaningful activities.
  • To apply reading strategies in professional contexts.
  • To understand purpose, audience and context in writing.
  • To develop analytical thinking for problem solving.
  • To prepare for job applications and interviews.

UNIT I: MAKING COMPARISONS

Reading advertisements, manuals and brochures - Writing professional emails and compare & contrast essays - Grammar: mixed tenses, prepositional phrases.

UNIT II: EXPRESSING CAUSAL RELATIONS

Reading technical texts and complaint letters - Writing responses and cause-effect essays - Grammar: active/passive voice, infinitives and gerunds.

UNIT III: PROBLEM SOLVING

Reading case studies and reports - Writing letters to editor, checklists and essays - Grammar: error correction, conditional sentences.

UNIT IV: REPORTING EVENTS AND RESEARCH

Reading articles - Writing reports, recommendations and surveys - Grammar: reported speech, modals - Vocabulary: conjunctions, prepositions.

UNIT V: EXPRESSING IDEAS CLEARLY

Reading company profiles and SOPs - Writing job applications, resumes and cover letters - Grammar: numerical adjectives, relative clauses.

COURSE OUTCOMES

At the end of the course, learners will be able to:

  • CO1: Compare and contrast ideas in technical texts.
  • CO2: Identify cause-effect relationships.
  • CO3: Solve problems and communicate solutions.
  • CO4: Present ideas logically.
  • CO5: Create effective resumes.

TEXT BOOKS

  1. English for Engineers & Technologists, Orient Blackswan, 2020.
  2. English for Science & Technology, Cambridge University Press, 2021.
  3. Authored by Anna University Faculty.

REFERENCES

  1. Meenakshi Raman & Sangeeta Sharma, Professional English, 2019.
  2. V.N. Arora, Improve Your Writing, 2001.
  3. Dr. V. Chellammal, Learning to Communicate, 2003.
  4. R.C. Sharma & Krishna Mohan, Business Correspondence, 2001.
  5. Krishna Mohan & Meera Bannerji, Communication Skills, 1990.

ASSESSMENT PATTERN

Two internal assessments and one end semester examination evaluating reading, writing, grammar and vocabulary skills.
