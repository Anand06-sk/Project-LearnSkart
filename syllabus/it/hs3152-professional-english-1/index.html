HS3152 PROFESSIONAL ENGLISH I

L T P C: 3 0 0 3 | Total: 45 Periods

COURSE OBJECTIVES

  • To improve communicative competence.
  • To learn basic grammatical structures in context.
  • To acquire and use vocabulary effectively.
  • To use language in professional contexts.
  • To develop reading and writing skills for various formats.

UNIT I: INTRODUCTION TO EFFECTIVE COMMUNICATION

Basics of effective communication - Seven C’s - Language skills (listening, speaking, reading, writing) - Reading brochures, messages and emails - Writing self-introduction emails - Grammar: present tense, question types - Vocabulary: synonyms, abbreviations, acronyms.

UNIT II: NARRATION AND SUMMATION

Reading biographies, reports and blogs - Paragraph writing and short reports - Grammar: past tense, subject-verb agreement, prepositions - Vocabulary: prefixes, suffixes, synonyms, antonyms, phrasal verbs.

UNIT III: DESCRIPTION OF PROCESS / PRODUCT

Reading ads, reviews, manuals - Writing definitions and descriptions - Grammar: imperatives, adjectives, comparison, perfect tenses - Vocabulary: compound nouns, homonyms, discourse markers.

UNIT IV: CLASSIFICATION AND RECOMMENDATIONS

Reading articles and charts - Note-making and recommendations - Data interpretation - Grammar: articles, pronouns - Vocabulary: collocations and fixed expressions.

UNIT V: EXPRESSION

Reading editorials and blogs - Essay writing - Grammar: future tense, punctuation, sentence structures - Vocabulary: cause & effect expressions.

COURSE OUTCOMES

At the end of the course, learners will be able to:

  • CO1: Use appropriate vocabulary in professional contexts.
  • CO2: Apply grammatical structures correctly.
  • CO3: Understand technical texts effectively.
  • CO4: Write various forms like essays and descriptions.

TEXT BOOKS

  1. English for Engineers & Technologists, Orient Blackswan, 2020.
  2. English for Science & Technology, Cambridge University Press, 2021.

REFERENCES

  1. Meenakshi Raman & Sangeeta Sharma, Technical Communication, 2016.
  2. Lakshminarayanan, Technical English.
  3. Aysha Viswamohan, English for Technical Communication.
  4. Kulbhusan Kumar, Effective Communication Skill.
  5. Dr. V. Chellammal, Learning to Communicate.

ASSESSMENT PATTERN

Two internal assessments and one end semester exam focusing on reading, writing, grammar and vocabulary skills.
