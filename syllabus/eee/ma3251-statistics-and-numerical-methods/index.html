MA3251 STATISTICS AND NUMERICAL METHODS

L T P C: 3 1 0 4 | Total: 60 Periods

COURSE OBJECTIVES

  • To understand statistical and numerical methods used in engineering problems.
  • To learn hypothesis testing for small and large samples.
  • To solve algebraic and transcendental equations numerically.
  • To apply interpolation, differentiation and integration techniques.
  • To solve ordinary differential equations using numerical methods.

UNIT I: TESTING OF HYPOTHESIS

Sampling distributions - Tests for mean, proportion and difference of means - Tests for variance - Chi-square test - Independence of attributes.

UNIT II: DESIGN OF EXPERIMENTS

One-way and two-way classifications - Completely randomized design - Randomized block design - Latin square design - Factorial design.

UNIT III: SOLUTION OF EQUATIONS AND EIGENVALUE PROBLEMS

Numerical solutions of equations - Newton-Raphson method - Linear systems (Gauss methods) - Iterative methods - Eigenvalues using power and Jacobi methods.

UNIT IV: INTERPOLATION AND NUMERICAL METHODS

Lagrange and Newton interpolation - Numerical differentiation - Numerical integration using Trapezoidal and Simpson’s rules.

UNIT V: NUMERICAL SOLUTION OF DIFFERENTIAL EQUATIONS

Euler methods - Runge-Kutta methods - Predictor-corrector methods (Milne’s and Adams-Bashforth).

COURSE OUTCOMES

Upon completion, students will be able to:

  • CO1: Apply hypothesis testing in real-world problems.
  • CO2: Use experimental design techniques.
  • CO3: Apply numerical interpolation and integration methods.
  • CO4: Solve differential equations numerically.
  • CO5: Apply numerical methods in engineering applications.

TEXT BOOKS

  1. Grewal B.S., "Numerical Methods in Engineering and Science", 2015.
  2. Johnson R.A., Miller I., Freund J., "Probability and Statistics for Engineers", 2015.
