GE3791 HUMAN VALUES AND ETHICS

L T P C: 2 0 0 2 | Total: 30 Periods

COURSE DESCRIPTION

This course provides an understanding of modern values and ethical principles embedded in the Constitution of India, focusing on democratic, secular and scientific aspects. It helps students apply these values in daily life.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

  • To create awareness about values and ethics in the Constitution of India.
  • To sensitize students about democratic values in modern society.
  • To inculcate respect for all people irrespective of differences.
  • To develop scientific temper and critical thinking.
  • To promote responsibility and duties of citizens.

UNIT I: DEMOCRATIC VALUES

Equality, Liberty, Fraternity, Justice, Freedom, Pluralism, Tolerance - Citizen participation in governance - World democracies: French Revolution, American Independence, Indian Freedom Movement - Reading: John Stuart Mill's "On Liberty".

UNIT II: SECULAR VALUES

Secularism in Indian context - Separation of state and religion - Acceptance of all faiths - Non-discrimination - Reading: Ram Puniyani on secularism.

UNIT III: SCIENTIFIC VALUES

Scientific method - Inductive & deductive reasoning - Hypothesis testing - Evidence-based validation - Rationalism, empiricism and scientific temper.

UNIT IV: SOCIAL ETHICS

Ethical reasoning in social issues - Gender bias and violence - Social discrimination - Constitutional protections - Inclusive practices - Reading: Yuval Noah Harari.

UNIT V: SCIENTIFIC ETHICS

Ethics in science - Transparency and fairness - Responsible use of scientific inventions - Role of scientists in society.

COURSE OUTCOMES

Students will be able to:

  • CO1: Understand democratic, secular and scientific values.
  • CO2: Apply democratic and scientific values in life.
  • CO3: Solve social problems using rational thinking.
  • CO4: Act ethically in society.
  • CO5: Practice critical thinking.

TEXT BOOKS

No separate textbooks were listed in the provided syllabus.

REFERENCES

  1. Luke W. Galen, "The Nonreligious", 2016.
  2. Bullivant & Lee, "Secularism: A Dictionary of Atheism", 2016.
  3. John R. Shook, "The Oxford Handbook of Secularism", 2017.
  4. Almond & Verba, "The Civic Culture".
  5. Soumitro Banerjee, "Research Methodology for Natural Sciences", 2022.
