GE3451 ENVIRONMENTAL SCIENCES AND SUSTAINABILITY

L T P C: 2 0 0 2 | Total: 30 Periods

COURSE OBJECTIVES

  • To introduce the basic concepts of environment, ecosystems and biodiversity and emphasize on biodiversity conservation.
  • To impart knowledge on causes, effects and prevention of environmental pollution and natural disasters.
  • To understand renewable and non-renewable resources and methods to preserve them.
  • To understand sustainable development goals, climate change, carbon credit and environmental challenges.
  • To promote sustainability practices and understanding of green materials, energy cycles and urban sustainability.

UNIT I: ENVIRONMENT AND BIODIVERSITY

Definition, scope and importance of environment - Ecosystem and energy flow - Ecological succession - Types of biodiversity - Values of biodiversity - India as a mega-diversity nation - Biodiversity hotspots - Threats and conservation methods (in-situ & ex-situ).

UNIT II: ENVIRONMENTAL POLLUTION

Causes, effects and prevention of water, soil, air and noise pollution - Waste management (solid, hazardous, e-waste) - Occupational health and safety - Environmental protection acts.

UNIT III: RENEWABLE SOURCES OF ENERGY

Energy management and conservation - New energy sources - Hydrogen energy, ocean energy, tidal energy - Geothermal energy and its applications.

UNIT IV: SUSTAINABILITY AND MANAGEMENT

Sustainability concepts - Economic and social aspects - Sustainable Development Goals - Climate change issues and solutions - Carbon credit and footprint - Environmental management in industry.

UNIT V: SUSTAINABILITY PRACTICES

Zero waste and circular economy - ISO 14000 - Life cycle assessment - Environmental impact assessment - Green buildings and materials - Sustainable transport and energy - Urban sustainability.

COURSE OUTCOMES

  • CO1: Understand environment, ecosystems and biodiversity.
  • CO2: Identify causes and effects of pollution and preventive measures.
  • CO3: Apply knowledge of renewable and non-renewable resources.
  • CO4: Understand sustainable development goals and environmental challenges.
  • CO5: Apply sustainability practices and concepts of green engineering.

TEXT BOOKS

  2. Benny Joseph, "Environmental Science and Engineering", 2016.
  3. Gilbert M. Masters, "Introduction to Environmental Engineering and Science", 2004.
  4. Allen D.T. & Shonnard D.R., "Sustainability Engineering".
  5. Bradley A.S. et al., "Engineering Applications in Sustainable Design".
  6. Government of India, "Environmental Impact Assessment Guidelines", 2006.
  7. Mackenthun K.M., "Environmental Management", 1998.
