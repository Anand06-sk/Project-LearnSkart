PH3151 ENGINEERING PHYSICS

L T P C: 3 0 0 3 | Total: 45 Periods

COURSE OBJECTIVES

  • To understand the fundamentals of mechanics.
  • To learn electromagnetic waves and their applications.
  • To study oscillations, optics and lasers.
  • To understand the basics of quantum physics.
  • To explore applications of quantum mechanics.

UNIT I: MECHANICS

Centre of mass and motion - Rotational dynamics - Moment of inertia - Angular momentum - Gyroscope - Pendulums - Introduction to nonlinear oscillations.

UNIT II: ELECTROMAGNETIC WAVES

Maxwell’s equations - EM wave properties - Polarization - Energy and momentum - Radiation pressure - Reflection and transmission.

UNIT III: OSCILLATIONS, OPTICS AND LASERS

Simple harmonic motion - Waves and sound - Doppler effect - Interference - Michelson interferometer - Lasers and their applications.

UNIT IV: BASIC QUANTUM MECHANICS

Photons and matter waves - Schrödinger equation - Wave function - Particle in a box - Quantum states and probabilities.

UNIT V: APPLIED QUANTUM MECHANICS

Harmonic oscillator - Quantum tunneling - Applications like tunneling microscope and diodes - Energy bands and Kronig-Penney model.

COURSE OUTCOMES

After completion, students will be able to:

  • CO1: Understand mechanics concepts.
  • CO2: Explain electromagnetic waves.
  • CO3: Apply concepts of optics and lasers.
  • CO4: Understand quantum physics principles.
  • CO5: Apply quantum mechanics in real-world systems.

TEXT BOOKS

  1. Kleppner D., Kolenkow R., "Introduction to Mechanics", 2017.
  2. Purcell E.M., Morin D.J., "Electricity and Magnetism", 2013.
  3. Beiser A., "Concepts of Modern Physics", 2017.
