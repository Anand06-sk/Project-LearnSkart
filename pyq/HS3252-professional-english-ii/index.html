Important Questions - HS3252 Professional English-II

Part A (2 Marks – Grammar & Vocabulary)

  • Fill in the blanks using suitable conjunctions (although, because, despite, etc.)
  • Choose the correct preposition / collocation
  • Identify or form relative clauses and conditional sentences
  • Give meanings or use phrasal verbs in sentences
  • Use suitable idioms in professional context
  • Correct the errors (tense, subject–verb agreement, sentence structure)

Part B (13 / 16 Marks – Writing Skills)

  • Resume & Cover Letter: Prepare a resume (functional / chronological) with given details; Write a cover letter for a job application
  • Report Writing: Write a feasibility report; Write a survey report based on given data; Write an accident / incident report
  • Letter Writing: Write a complaint letter; Write a claim / adjustment letter; Write an enquiry letter
  • Essay & Checklist: Write an essay on a given topic (technology, environment, etc.); Prepare a checklist for a given situation
  • Professional Communication: Draft agenda and minutes of meeting (MOM); Write guidelines for effective presentations

Part C (Case Study / Comprehension)

  • Read a passage and answer detailed questions
  • Summarize a given passage
  • Case study based on professional communication scenario

Most Repeated / High-Weight Questions

Technical report writing, formal business emails, presentation skills, group discussion participation, editing and proofreading, professional correspondence, workplace communication scenarios, resume writing, cover letter writing.

Frequently Asked Questions about HS3252 Question Papers

What types of technical documents are asked in HS3252 exams?

Technical reports, proposals, instruction manuals, abstracts, email correspondence, and memoranda are commonly asked. Practice writing each type from previous year papers to understand expected format, content, and professional tone.

How should I prepare for HS3252 presentation and GD questions?

Practice organizing technical content for presentations, create visual aid sketches, participate in mock group discussions, and study etiquette guidelines from previous papers. Record yourself presenting to identify improvement areas.

Which HS3252 topics appear most frequently in exams?

Technical report writing, formal email drafting, presentation skills, group discussion participation, and editing/proofreading are high-frequency topics. Allocate more preparation time to these topics as they repeat consistently.

How do I improve technical writing quality for HS3252?

Write drafts, self-edit using checklists (clarity, conciseness, grammar), compare with model answers in previous papers, seek feedback on structure and tone. Practice regularly using different technical scenarios to develop consistency.

Are HS3252 previous year papers enough for workplace communication skills?

Question papers provide exam patterns and practice opportunities, but workplace skills develop through consistent writing and presentation practice. Use papers to learn formats, then apply skills in real-world scenarios for practical competence.
