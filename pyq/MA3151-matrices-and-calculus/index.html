Important Questions - MA3151 Matrices and Calculus

UNIT 1 - Matrices

Part A

  • State Cayley-Hamilton theorem
  • Find sum and product of eigenvalues
  • Determine nature of quadratic form
  • If lambda is eigenvalue of A, find eigenvalues of A2, A-1

Part B

  • Find eigenvalues and eigenvectors of a matrix
  • Diagonalize a matrix using orthogonal transformation
  • Reduce quadratic form to canonical form
  • Use Cayley-Hamilton theorem to find A2, A-1

UNIT 2 - Differential Calculus

Part A

  • Find domain and range of a function
  • Test continuity/discontinuity
  • State fundamental theorem of calculus
  • Differentiate using chain rule / implicit differentiation

Part B

  • Find maxima and minima
  • Find equation of tangent and normal
  • Verify Rolle's theorem / Mean Value theorem
  • Solve application problems (velocity, rate of change)

UNIT 3 - Functions of Several Variables

Part A

  • Find Jacobian
  • State properties of Jacobians
  • Expand Taylor series (first order)
  • Find stationary points

Part B

  • Find maxima and minima of two variables
  • Solve using Lagrange multipliers
  • Expand Taylor series (higher order)
  • Solve total derivative / change of variables

UNIT 4 - Integral Calculus

Part A

  • Evaluate basic integrals
  • Test convergence of improper integrals
  • Apply integration by parts

Part B

  • Solve using partial fractions
  • Solve using trigonometric substitution
  • Find area between curves
  • Solve centre of mass / moment problems

UNIT 5 - Multiple Integrals

Part A

  • Evaluate double/triple integrals
  • Formula for volume using triple integrals
  • Define change of order of integration

Part B

  • Change order of integration and evaluate
  • Find area using double integrals
  • Solve using polar coordinates
  • Find volume using triple integrals

Most Repeated / High-Weight Questions

Eigenvalues and eigenvectors, matrix diagonalization, quadratic forms, maxima/minima problems, Jacobians, Taylor series, Rolle's and Mean Value theorems, double/triple integrals, change of order of integration, area and volume calculations.

Frequently Asked Questions about MA3151 Question Papers

Which MA3151 units have the most numerical problems?

Units 1 (Eigenvalues, Cayley-Hamilton), 3 (Jacobian, Lagrange multipliers), and 4-5 (Integration problems) are highly numerical. Practice solving varied numerical problems from previous year papers to build speed and accuracy in calculations.

How should I approach Cayley-Hamilton theorem problems in MA3151?

Understand the theorem statement: every square matrix satisfies its own characteristic equation. Practice finding characteristic polynomials, then use them to calculate powers and inverses. Solve multiple examples from previous papers to identify patterns and shortcuts.

What are the most frequently asked MA3151 topics?

Eigenvalues and eigenvectors, quadratic form reduction, Lagrange multipliers, double and triple integrals, and mean value theorem are high-frequency topics. Focus preparation on these areas as they repeat consistently across years.

How do I solve double integral problems efficiently in MA3151?

Start by sketching the region of integration, decide the order of integration, convert to appropriate coordinates (Cartesian/polar), then evaluate step-by-step. Practice changing order of integration and using symmetry properties to simplify calculations.

Are Taylor series expansion questions common in MA3151?

Yes, both first-order and higher-order Taylor series appear regularly. Practice expanding functions of one and two variables. Use previous year papers to identify commonly expanded functions and memorize standard expansions for quick problem-solving.

How can MA3151 previous papers help with exam confidence?

Solve complete previous year papers within the exam time limit. This builds confidence, improves time management, and helps identify weak topics needing more practice before the actual exam.
