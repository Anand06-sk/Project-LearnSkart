Important Questions - GE3791 Human Values and Ethics

UNIT I: Democratic Values

Part A (2 Marks)

  • Define democratic values and list their key aspects (e.g., equality, liberty, fraternity).
  • What is the difference between intrinsic and extrinsic human values?.
  • State the importance of the separation of powers in a democracy.
  • Define "tolerance" and its role in a democratic society.

Part B (13/16 Marks)

  • Core Principles: Discuss the principles of democracy, including equality, justice, and citizen participation.
  • Historical Movements: Analyze how the French Revolution, American Independence, and the Indian Freedom Movement shaped modern democratic values.
  • Liberty: Provide a detailed explanation of John Stuart Mill's concepts presented in On Liberty.

UNIT II: Secular Values

Part A (2 Marks)

  • Define secular values and explain "religious neutrality".
  • What is the principle of Sarva Dharma Sambhava?.
  • List the benefits of the disassociation of state from religion.

Part B (13/16 Marks)

  • Indian Secularism: Discuss the interpretation, features, and status of secularism in the Indian context vs. Western models.
  • Non-Discriminatory Practices: Explain the importance and ways to encourage non-discriminatory behaviors in society.
  • Challenges: Analyze the contemporary threats and challenges to secular values in India.

UNIT III: Scientific Values

Part A (2 Marks)

  • Define scientific thinking and the term "scientific temper".
  • Distinguish between inductive and deductive reasoning.
  • What are the benefits of skepticism and empiricism in research?.

Part B (13/16 Marks)

  • Scientific Method: Detail the steps involved in proposing and testing a hypothesis.
  • Rationalism: Explain the role of rationalism and evidence-based validation in advancing scientific knowledge.

UNIT IV: Social Issues and Ethics

Part A (2 Marks)

  • Define gender bias and gender-based violence.
  • What is "intersectionality" in the context of social discrimination?.
  • Name key constitutional protections for human rights in India.

Part B (13/16 Marks)

  • Gender Equality: Discuss the causes, impacts, and ethical considerations for reducing gender bias in society.
  • Social Well-being: Analyze how ethics and inclusive practices contribute to collective human well-being.

UNIT V: Professional and Research Ethics

Part A (2 Marks)

  • Define transparency and accountability in scientific research.
  • Why is informed consent critical in research involving human subjects?.
  • What does avoiding plagiarism ensure in professional ethics?.

Part B (13/16 Marks)

  • Research Integrity: Discuss the importance of fairness, objectivity, and addressing conflicts of interest in research.
  • Social Responsibility: Explain the ethical responsibility of scientists and professionals toward society.

Most Repeated / High-Weight Questions

Democratic principles and historical movements, Indian secularism and religious neutrality, scientific temper and evidence-based reasoning, gender equality and social discrimination, research ethics and professional responsibility.

How to Use These Question Papers

  • Democratic Foundations: Understand core principles: equality, liberty, fraternity, justice, citizen participation. Study historical movements and their impact on modern values. Analyze separation of powers importance.
  • Indian Context Focus: Compare Indian secularism with Western models. Understand Sarva Dharma Sambhava and religious neutrality. Study constitutional protections and human rights safeguards specific to India.
  • Scientific Temper: Master the scientific method: hypothesis, observation, experimentation, conclusion. Distinguish inductive (specific to general) and deductive (general to specific) reasoning. Understand role of skepticism and empiricism.
  • Ethics & Social Issues: Study gender bias causes and impacts. Understand intersectionality in discrimination. Know professional ethics: informed consent, plagiarism avoidance, transparency, accountability, conflicts of interest.
  • Time Management: Allocate 60-90 minutes per ethical analysis problem; practice essays on values and case studies under timed conditions combining philosophical and practical aspects.

Frequently Asked Questions about GE3791 Human Values and Ethics

Which topics are most important in GE3791 exams?

Democratic principles with historical movements (Unit I), Indian secularism and religious neutrality (Unit II), scientific temper and scientific method (Unit III), gender equality and social discrimination (Unit IV), and research ethics with professional responsibility (Unit V) are equally weighted. Focus on Indian constitutional context and practical ethical scenarios.

How should I approach democratic values questions in GE3791?

Understand trinity of democracy: equality, liberty, fraternity. Study French Revolution (1789), American Independence (1776), and Indian Freedom Movement (1947) historical impacts. Discuss separation of powers and checks-and-balances. Analyze John Stuart Mill's "On Liberty" philosophy on individual freedom.

What is the best strategy for Indian secularism questions in GE3791?

Understand Indian Constitution's secular character and religious neutrality principle. Know Sarva Dharma Sambhava (equal respect for all religions) concept. Compare with Western secular models. Study constitutional articles protecting human rights (Articles 14-28). Analyze contemporary secularism challenges in India.

How can I master scientific values in GE3791?

Master scientific method steps: problem identification, hypothesis formulation, experimental design, data collection, analysis, conclusion. Distinguish inductive reasoning (observations to general principle) from deductive reasoning (general principle to specific case). Understand value of skepticism and empiricism in research validation.

What should I know about gender equality and social issues in GE3791?

Define gender bias and gender-based violence. Understand intersectionality concept (overlapping discrimination factors). Study causes and societal impacts of gender discrimination. Know constitutional protections (Articles 14, 15, 16, 39) and legal frameworks. Discuss ethical solutions for inclusive society.

How should I handle research ethics and professional responsibility in GE3791?

Understand research integrity principles: fairness, objectivity, transparency, accountability. Know importance of informed consent in human subject research. Master plagiarism avoidance and intellectual property respect. Study conflict of interest identification and resolution. Discuss scientists' social responsibility toward sustainable development.
