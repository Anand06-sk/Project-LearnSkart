Important Questions - HS3152 Professional English-I

PART A – More Question Types

  • Fill in the blanks with correct modal verb
  • Choose the correct phrasal verb
  • Rewrite the sentence using comparative/superlative form
  • Combine two sentences using suitable linkers
  • Identify the error and correct it
  • Rearrange words to form a meaningful sentence
  • Expand the given abbreviation
  • Match words with their meanings
  • Choose the correct technical term
  • Answer very short questions based on a short paragraph

PART B – Final High-Scoring Question Pattern

  • Write a paragraph (150–200 words) on a general/technical topic
  • Write an essay (250–300 words)
  • Write a formal letter (complaint / enquiry / application)
  • Draft a professional email (formal situation)
  • Prepare a resume using given data
  • Write a cover letter for job application
  • Comprehension passage – answer detailed questions
  • Summarize a given passage
  • Write a report (event / incident / data-based)
  • Explain communication concepts with examples (barriers, GD, interview, etc.)

Important Correction (Don't Miss This)

Some papers don't ask both paragraph AND essay together. Usually: Either paragraph OR essay OR essay + report/email-type question. So don't overprepare both equally—focus more on: Essay, Email / Letter, Resume + Cover Letter, Report.

Most Repeated / High Weight Questions

Focus here if time is less: Resume + Cover Letter (very frequent), Formal Email / Letter, Report Writing, Comprehension + Summary, Essay (general topics like technology, education, environment).

How to Use These Question Papers

  • Practice Writing Daily: Spend 30-45 minutes on Part B essays, letters, and resumes using past papers to build speed and accuracy.
  • Grammar Drills: Focus on Part A questions involving modal verbs, phrasal verbs, and error identification for quick revision.
  • Timed Practice: Simulate exam conditions by timing yourself; allocate 20 minutes for Part A (objective) and 25-30 minutes for Part B (writing).
  • Recurring Patterns: Note that resume+cover letter combinations appear frequently—prioritize this over essay alone.
  • Comprehension Strategy: Read each passage twice—once for overall meaning, then analyze specific lines for detailed answers.

Frequently Asked Questions about HS3152 Question Papers

What are the main topics covered in HS3152 Professional English-I?

HS3152 covers grammar fundamentals (modal verbs, phrasal verbs, error correction), writing skills (formal letters, emails, resumes, cover letters), comprehension, and communication concepts. The course emphasizes professional communication and technical writing for engineering students.

How should I prepare Part B (writing tasks) for HS3152 exams?

Practice writing 150-300 word paragraphs and essays on general/technical topics regularly. Use past papers to understand the expected format for formal letters, professional emails, and resumes. Time yourself to complete all tasks within 25-30 minutes during exam conditions.

Which topics appear most frequently in HS3152 previous year papers?

Resume + cover letter combinations, formal letter writing (complaint/enquiry/application), professional email drafting, and comprehension with summary questions are the most repeated. Focus your preparation on these high-frequency topics for better exam scores.

How are HS3152 question papers structured?

Part A (2-3 marks) contains objective questions on grammar, vocabulary, and sentence correction. Part B (10-15 marks) requires extended writing such as essays, letters, resumes, and reports. Time allocation: 20 minutes for Part A and 25-30 minutes for Part B is recommended.

Are there any tips for improving comprehension answers in HS3152?

Read passages twice—first for overall understanding, then carefully for specific details. Underline key words and answer based on the passage content only. Practice with previous year comprehension questions to identify common question patterns and answer types.

Can I improve my grammar using HS3152 question papers?

Yes. Part A questions specifically target grammar topics like modal verbs, phrasal verbs, and error identification. Practicing these repeatedly helps strengthen foundational grammar skills essential for technical communication in engineering.
