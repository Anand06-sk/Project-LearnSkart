Important Questions - GE3451 Environmental Sciences and Sustainability

UNIT I: Environment and Biodiversity

Part A (2 Marks)

  • Define Ecosystem and list its structural components.
  • What is Ecological Succession?
  • Define Biodiversity and its three levels (Genetic, Species, Ecosystem).
  • What are Endemic Species? Give an example.
  • Distinguish between Food Chain and Food Web.

Part B (13 Marks)

  • Explain the structure and functional components of an Forest or Aquatic ecosystem.
  • Describe the energy flow in an ecosystem using the 10% law and energy pyramids.
  • Discuss the threats to biodiversity (Habitat loss, Poaching, Invasive species).
  • Compare In-situ (National parks, Sanctuaries) and Ex-situ (Zoos, Seed banks) conservation methods.

UNIT II: Environmental Pollution

Part A (2 Marks)

  • Define BOD (Biochemical Oxygen Demand) and COD.
  • What is the 3R concept in waste management?
  • Define E-waste and mention its hazards.
  • Name two Air Pollutants and their specific effects on human health.
  • What is Noise Pollution? Give its unit of measurement.

Part B (13 Marks)

  • Explain the causes, effects, and control measures of Air Pollution.
  • Describe the various stages involved in Municipal Sewage Treatment (Primary, Secondary, Tertiary).
  • Discuss the methods of Solid Waste Management (Landfilling, Incineration, Composting).
  • Explain the Occupational Health and Safety (OHSAS) measures in an industrial environment.

UNIT III: Renewable Sources of Energy

Part A (2 Marks)

  • What is Geothermal Energy?
  • Define Bio-mass and list two examples.
  • What are Hydrogen Fuel Cells?
  • Define Energy Management.
  • What is the significance of Tidal Energy?

Part B (13 Marks)

  • Explain the principle, construction, and working of a Solar Cell (Photovoltaic).
  • Discuss the production of electricity from Wind Energy and its pros/cons.
  • Describe the process of Ocean Thermal Energy Conversion (OTEC).
  • Explain the importance and techniques of Energy Conservation in daily life.

UNIT IV: Sustainability and Management

Part A (2 Marks)

  • Define Sustainable Development.
  • What is a Carbon Footprint?
  • List any four Millennium Development Goals (MDGs).
  • What is Carbon Sequestration?
  • Define Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA).

Part B (13 Marks)

  • Explain the three pillars of sustainability: Economic, Social, and Environmental.
  • Discuss the causes and global impacts of Climate Change and Global Warming.
  • Describe the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) set by the UN.
  • Explain the concept of Carbon Credits and how they help in pollution control.

UNIT V: Sustainability Practices

Part A (2 Marks)

  • Define Circular Economy.
  • What are Green Materials? Give examples.
  • What is Life Cycle Assessment (LCA)?
  • Define Zero Waste concept.
  • What is the purpose of ISO 14001?

Part B (13 Marks)

  • Explain the principles and features of Green Buildings (Sustainable Architecture).
  • Discuss the importance and stages of Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) of a product.
  • Explain the transition from a Linear Economy to a Circular Economy.
  • Discuss the role of Environmental Audit in achieving sustainability in industries.

Most Repeated / High-Weight Questions

Ecosystem structure and energy flow, biodiversity conservation, water and air pollution control measures, renewable energy sources, sustainable development goals, climate change impacts, life cycle assessment.

How to Use These Question Papers

  • Unit-Wise Preparation: Complete Unit I for ecosystem foundation, dedicate 30% of time to pollution control (Unit II) and 25% to energy sources (Unit III). Units IV-V focus on sustainability and practical applications.
  • Pollution Control Understanding: Understand BOD/COD calculations, sewage treatment stages (primary, secondary, tertiary), and waste management methods. These pollution control questions appear frequently with 13-15 marks.
  • Energy Sources Practice: Study solar cells, wind energy, geothermal, and ocean energy principles with diagrams. Compare advantages and disadvantages. Understand energy conservation techniques.
  • Sustainability Concepts: Master Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), carbon footprints, life cycle assessment stages, and circular economy principles. These conceptual questions appear with 13-15 marks.
  • Time Management: Allocate 60-90 minutes per environmental problem; practice Part B solutions combining multiple units on integrated sustainability topics.

Frequently Asked Questions about GE3451 Environmental Sciences and Sustainability

Which topics are most frequently tested in GE3451 exams?

Ecosystem structure and energy flow (Unit I), pollution control measures (Unit II), renewable energy sources (Unit III), and sustainable development goals (Unit IV) together account for 70% of exam marks. Unit V covers practical sustainability implementations. Master biodiversity conservation, sewage treatment, and energy alternatives.

How should I approach ecosystem and food chain questions in GE3451?

Understand ecosystem structure (biotic and abiotic factors). Distinguish food chains (linear) from food webs (complex networks). Explain energy flow using the 10% law with diagrams. Trace nutrient cycles for carbon, nitrogen, and phosphorus. Practice drawing different pyramid types (numbers, biomass, energy).

What is the best strategy for pollution control questions in GE3451?

Understand causes, effects, and control measures for air, water, and soil pollution separately. Study sewage treatment stages in detail (primary, secondary, tertiary). Know different waste management methods: landfilling, incineration, composting. These pollution control problems appear with 13-15 marks regularly.

How can I master renewable energy topics in GE3451?

Understand working principles of solar cells (photovoltaic effect), wind turbines (kinetic energy conversion), geothermal systems, and ocean energy (tidal, OTEC). Draw diagrams for each energy source. Compare pros/cons for each technology. Practice calculating energy efficiency and output.

What should I know about sustainable development in GE3451?

Master the definition and three pillars of sustainability (economic, social, environmental). Understand UN Sustainable Development Goals (17 SDGs) with specific focus on poverty, energy, climate, and industry. Know carbon credits mechanism and climate change impacts globally. These sustainability concepts appear with 13-15 marks.

How should I approach life cycle assessment questions in GE3451?

Understand LCA stages: goal definition, inventory analysis, impact assessment, interpretation. Grasp the concept of cradle-to-grave analysis. Understand circular economy transition from linear model. Recognize role of environmental audits in achieving industry sustainability. Practice with real product LCA case studies.
