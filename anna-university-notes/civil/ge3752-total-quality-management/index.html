Frequently Asked Questions

What is Total Quality Management and what are its core principles?
TQM is management philosophy focused on continuous improvement, customer satisfaction, and employee involvement. Core principles include customer focus, continuous improvement, leadership commitment, employee empowerment, and process orientation for organizational excellence.

How do Deming and Juran philosophies differ?
Deming emphasizes continuous improvement through statistical methods and eliminates fear; Juran focuses on planning, control, and improvement (Trilogy) with systematic quality management. Both advocate management responsibility in quality transformation.

What are the seven QC tools and when are they used?
Tools include check sheets (data collection), histograms (distribution), Pareto charts (prioritization), scatter plots (correlation), control charts (monitoring), fishbone diagrams (cause analysis), and flow charts (process mapping).

How is Six Sigma different from general quality improvement?
Six Sigma is structured methodology targeting 3.4 defects per million opportunities, using DMAIC (Define-Measure-Analyze-Improve-Control) approach with statistical rigor. It's more focused and quantitative than general TQM continuous improvement.

What are ISO 9001 requirements and ISO 14001 environmental standards?
ISO 9001 requires documented quality management system with process approach, management commitment, and continual improvement. ISO 14001 requires environmental management system addressing environmental aspects, legal compliance, and pollution prevention.

How should I prepare for GE3752 exams?
Focus on TQM concepts and Deming philosophy; master seven QC tools and their applications; understand continuous improvement methods (PDSA, Kaizen); study quality circles, QFD, FMEA; prepare ISO 9001/14001 requirements; solve application-based problems on quality management.
