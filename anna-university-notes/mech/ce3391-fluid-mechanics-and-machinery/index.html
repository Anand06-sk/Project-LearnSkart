About CE3391 Fluid Mechanics and Machinery

CE3391 Fluid Mechanics and Machinery is a key subject for Mechanical Engineering students in Semester 3 at Anna University. This subject introduces you to the fundamental principles of fluid statics, fluid dynamics, and the operation of hydraulic machines. These CE3391 notes are designed to help you understand the behavior of fluids and the working of pumps and turbines through clear explanations and practical examples.

With these Anna University study materials, you will learn to analyze fluid flow, apply Bernoulli’s equation, and understand the performance characteristics of hydraulic machinery. The content also covers dimensional analysis, similitude, and the selection of pumps and turbines for engineering applications. All resources are structured for progressive learning and exam preparation.

Important Topics

CE3391 – FLUID MECHANICS AND MACHINERY
IMPORTANT TOPICS (EXAM FOCUS)

Unit 1

  • Fluid properties (density, viscosity, etc.)
  • Fluid statics & pressure measurement
  • Buoyancy & floatation
  • Continuity equation
  • Bernoulli’s (energy) equation & applications

Unit 2

  • Reynolds experiment (laminar vs turbulent flow)
  • Darcy-Weisbach equation
  • Friction factor & Moody diagram
  • Major & minor losses in pipes
  • Pipes in series and parallel
  • Boundary layer concept

Unit 3

  • Buckingham π theorem
  • Dimensionless numbers (Re, Fr, etc.)
  • Model studies & similitude

Unit 4

  • Pelton wheel (very important)
  • Francis turbine & Kaplan turbine (basics + comparison)
  • Velocity triangles
  • Work done & efficiencies
  • Specific speed of turbines

Unit 5

  • Centrifugal pump (working + performance curves)
  • Heads & efficiencies of pumps
  • Reciprocating pump (working + indicator diagram)
  • Air vessels (concept)

🔥 Ultra Important (Must Focus)

  • Bernoulli’s equation & applications
  • Pipe flow losses + Moody diagram
  • Buckingham π theorem
  • Pelton wheel & turbine efficiencies
  • Centrifugal pump numericals
