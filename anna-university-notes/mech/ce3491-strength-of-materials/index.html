About CE3491 Strength of Materials

CE3491 Strength of Materials introduces students to the fundamental concepts of stress, strain, and deformation in engineering materials. The subject covers the analysis of beams, shafts, columns, and pressure vessels, providing a strong foundation for structural and mechanical design.

Students learn about elastic constants, bending, shear, torsion, and deflection methods, as well as the behavior of thin and thick cylinders. The course emphasizes practical problem-solving and the application of theory to real-world engineering challenges.

Mastery of these topics is essential for careers in civil, mechanical, and structural engineering, enabling students to design safe and efficient structures and components.

Important Topics

CE3491 Strength of Materials, unit-wise:

Unit 1
  • Stress & strain (tension, compression, shear)
  • Elastic constants & relationships
  • Thermal stresses
  • Principal stresses & planes
  • Mohr’s Circle

Unit 2
  • Shear force & bending moment (SFD & BMD)
  • Types of beams (cantilever, simply supported)
  • Bending stress distribution (simple bending theory)
  • Shear stress in beams
  • Flitched beams

Unit 3
  • Torsion of circular shafts (solid & hollow)
  • Power transmission in shafts
  • Combined bending & torsion
  • Helical springs (series & parallel)

Unit 4
  • Beam deflection methods:
  • Double integration
  • Macaulay’s method
  • Moment area method
  • Conjugate beam method

Unit 5
  • Thin cylinders (hoop & longitudinal stress)
  • Spherical shells
  • Thick cylinders
  • Lame’s Theory
