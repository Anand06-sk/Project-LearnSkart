About ME3492 Hydraulics and Pneumatics

ME3492 Hydraulics and Pneumatics introduces students to the principles and applications of fluid power systems in modern industry. The subject covers hydraulic and pneumatic components, circuits, and control strategies essential for automation and machinery.

Students learn about pumps, valves, actuators, accumulators, compressors, and circuit design for both hydraulic and pneumatic systems. The course emphasizes practical troubleshooting, maintenance, and the integration of electro-hydraulic and electro-pneumatic controls.

This knowledge is vital for careers in manufacturing, automation, and process industries, enabling students to design, analyze, and maintain efficient fluid power systems.

Important Topics

ME3492 Hydraulics and Pneumatics, unit-wise:

Unit 1
  • Hydraulic system basics (Pascal’s Law & applications)
  • Hydraulic pumps (vane pump – construction & working)
  • Hydraulic oil properties & selection criteria
  • Hydraulic system calculations (pressure, flow rate, efficiency, power)

Unit 2
  • Direction control valves (4/2, 4/3 valves – working & applications)
  • Pressure control valves & flow control valves (classification & uses)
  • Hydraulic actuators (cylinders & motors – performance, torque, efficiency)
  • Hydraulic circuits for industrial applications (e.g., punching machine)

Unit 3
  • Hydraulic accumulators (types & applications)
  • Hydraulic circuit design (regenerative, synchronous, fail-safe circuits)
  • Open-loop vs closed-loop hydraulic systems
  • Electro-hydraulic systems (servo valves & proportional valves)

Unit 4
  • Pneumatic systems vs electro-pneumatic systems
  • FRL unit (filter, regulator, lubricator – working)
  • Cascade method for pneumatic circuit design
  • Pneumatic sequential circuits (design & applications)

Unit 5
  • Hydraulic power pack (components & working)
  • Compressors & air treatment methods
  • Maintenance & troubleshooting of hydraulic and pneumatic systems
  • Industrial automation circuits (hydraulic drilling, safety systems)
