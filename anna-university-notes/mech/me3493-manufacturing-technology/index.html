About ME3493 Manufacturing Technology

ME3493 Manufacturing Technology covers the essential processes and machines used in modern manufacturing. The subject introduces students to machining, turning, grinding, milling, CNC, and computer-integrated manufacturing, building a strong foundation for production engineering.

Students learn about cutting tool materials, chip formation, lathe and milling operations, CNC programming, and automation. The course emphasizes practical problem-solving, process optimization, and the integration of advanced manufacturing technologies.

This knowledge is crucial for careers in manufacturing, production, and automation, enabling students to design, analyze, and improve manufacturing systems for higher efficiency and quality.

Important Topics

ME3493 Manufacturing Technology, unit-wise:

Unit 1
  • Machining fundamentals (orthogonal vs oblique cutting)
  • Chip formation & types of chips
  • Cutting tool materials & properties
  • Cutting fluids (types, functions, requirements)
  • Tool life & effect of temperature
  • Machinability concept

Unit 2
  • Lathe machine (construction & parts – compound rest, cross slide)
  • Turning operation (cutting forces & influencing factors)
  • Work holding devices in lathe
  • Machining time calculation (turning operations)
  • Types of lathe operations

Unit 3
  • Grinding process (wheel characteristics, truing & dressing)
  • Milling machine (types, cutters, up vs down milling)
  • Gear cutting (hobbing principle)
  • Shaper mechanism (quick return mechanism)
  • Centreless grinding (types of feed)

Unit 4
  • NC & CNC machines (basic concepts & need)
  • Open-loop vs closed-loop control systems
  • CNC machine components & working
  • Interpolation methods in CNC

Unit 5
  • Computer Integrated Manufacturing (CIM) basics
  • CNC programming (manual part programming basics)
  • Fixed cycles, loops & subroutines
  • Absolute vs incremental coordinate systems
