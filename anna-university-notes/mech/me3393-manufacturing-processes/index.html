About ME3393 Manufacturing Processes

ME3393 Manufacturing Processes is a core subject for mechanical engineering students, providing a comprehensive overview of how products are made from raw materials. The course covers traditional and modern manufacturing methods, helping students understand the principles behind casting, joining, forming, and plastics processing.

By learning about various manufacturing techniques, students gain practical knowledge of how different processes affect product quality, cost, and performance. This subject builds a strong foundation for future studies in production, design, and industrial engineering.

The knowledge gained in ME3393 is essential for anyone aiming to work in manufacturing, process planning, or quality control, as it bridges the gap between engineering theory and real-world production.

Important Topics

ME3393 Manufacturing Processes – Important Topics

Unit 1 – Casting
  • Sand casting process (steps & diagram)
  • Pattern types & pattern allowances
  • Moulding sand properties
  • Special casting methods
  • Die casting
  • Investment casting
  • Centrifugal casting
  • Casting defects & remedies

Unit 2 – Joining
  • Arc welding (principle & types)
  • Gas welding (oxy-fuel basics)
  • TIG & MIG welding (comparison important)
  • Resistance welding
  • Advanced welding (laser / electron beam – basics)
  • Weld defects & inspection methods
  • Brazing & soldering (difference)

Unit 3 – Bulk Deformation
  • Hot working vs Cold working
  • Forging (types & operations)
  • Rolling process (basic concept & defects)
  • Extrusion (types & working)
  • Drawing process (wire/rod basics)

Unit 4 – Sheet Metal
  • Shearing, bending & drawing operations
  • Formability of sheet metals
  • Stretch forming
  • Special forming processes (hydroforming, spinning – basics)

Unit 5 – Plastics Manufacturing
  • Types of plastics (thermoplastics vs thermosets)
  • Injection moulding (very important)
  • Compression moulding
  • Extrusion process
  • Blow moulding (basic idea)

🔥 Ultra Important (Must Focus)
If time is less, prioritize:
  • Sand casting + defects
  • Arc welding + TIG vs MIG
  • Forging & rolling
  • Injection moulding
  • Sheet metal operations
