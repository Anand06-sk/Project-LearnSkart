About ME3791 Mechatronics and IOT

ME3791 Mechatronics and IOT integrates mechanical systems with electronics, control systems, and digital technologies. This subject prepares mechanical engineers to design and implement smart systems, IoT devices, and automated solutions that are critical in modern industries.

These ME3791 notes cover sensors, actuators, microcontrollers, embedded systems, Arduino, Raspberry Pi, and IoT architecture. With our comprehensive study materials, you gain practical skills in interfacing hardware with software and building real-time IoT applications for industrial and consumer applications.

Master the blend of mechanical engineering with electronics and embedded systems, enabling you to develop innovative mechatronic solutions and IoT projects that drive automation and smart manufacturing.

Important Topics

Unit 1
  • Mechatronics concept and modular approach
  • Static and dynamic characteristics of sensors

  • Stepper motors (PM, VR, hybrid) and servo motors (DC & AC)

  • Real-time IoT applications and integration

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the main focus of ME3791 Mechatronics and IOT?
The subject integrates mechanical systems with electronics, embedded systems, and IoT technologies to create smart, automated solutions for modern industries.

Which sensors and actuators are most important?
Focus on resistive, capacitive, inductive, optical sensors, stepper motors, and servo motors as they form the foundation of mechatronic systems.

How do I program Arduino for IoT applications?
Our notes provide step-by-step Arduino programming examples using C++ for sensor interfacing and Wi-Fi connectivity with ESP8266 modules.

What is the difference between Arduino and Raspberry Pi?
Arduino is a microcontroller board for real-time control, while Raspberry Pi is a microcomputer suitable for complex IoT applications requiring OS and networking capabilities.

How should I prepare for ME3791 exams?
Study sensor characteristics, PLC ladder logic programming, Arduino interfacing, and practical IoT applications with emphasis on problem-solving and project-based understanding.
