About ME3591 Design of Machine Elements

ME3591 Design of Machine Elements teaches students the principles of designing safe and efficient machine components. The subject covers failure theories, fatigue, stress analysis, and the design of shafts, joints, springs, and bearings.

Students learn to apply design standards, select materials, and analyze combined stresses for real-world engineering applications. The course emphasizes practical design considerations, endurance, and reliability.

Mastery of these topics is essential for careers in mechanical design, manufacturing, and maintenance, enabling students to create robust and innovative machine elements.

Important Topics

ME3591 Design of Machine Elements, unit-wise:

Unit 1
  • Modes of failure & factor of safety
  • Theories of failure (maximum stress, strain, energy theories)
  • Combined stresses & principal stresses
  • Fatigue (endurance limit, fluctuating stresses, design for finite/infinite life)
  • Stress concentration & design considerations

Unit 2
  • Design of shafts (solid & hollow – strength & rigidity)
  • Torsion in shafts & power transmission
  • Keys and splines
  • Couplings (rigid & flexible – basic design concepts)

Unit 3
  • Bolted joints (including eccentric loading)
  • Knuckle & cotter joints
  • Welded joints (butt & fillet under different loads)
  • Riveted joints (basic design concepts)

Unit 4
  • Helical springs (design & stress analysis)
  • Laminated (leaf) springs
  • Flywheel design (energy storage & stresses)
  • Connecting rods & crankshafts (basic design concepts)

Unit 5
  • Hydrodynamic journal bearings
  • Bearing selection (rolling contact bearings)
  • Sommerfeld number & performance parameters
  • Seals and gaskets (basic design considerations)
