About ME3392 Engineering Materials and Metallurgy

ME3392 Engineering Materials and Metallurgy introduces students to the essential concepts of materials science, focusing on the structure, properties, and processing of engineering materials. This subject helps mechanical engineering students understand how different materials behave under various conditions and why material selection is critical in engineering design.

The course covers metals, alloys, polymers, ceramics, and composites, along with their heat treatment, mechanical properties, and failure mechanisms. By mastering these topics, students gain the knowledge needed to choose suitable materials for specific applications and to analyze the causes of material failures in real-world engineering scenarios.

With a strong foundation in metallurgy and materials, students are better prepared for advanced subjects and practical challenges in manufacturing, design, and quality control within the mechanical engineering field.

Important Topics

ME3392 Engineering Materials and Metallurgy – Important Topics

Unit 1
Phase diagrams (isomorphous & eutectic)
Eutectoid reaction (very important)
Iron–Iron carbide diagram
Solid solutions (substitutional & interstitial)
Classification of steels & cast irons (basics + properties)

Unit 2
  • Heat treatment processes
  • Annealing, normalizing, hardening, tempering
  • TTT diagram (Isothermal transformation)
  • CCT diagram (basic idea)
  • Hardenability & Jominy test
  • Case hardening methods (carburizing, nitriding)

Unit 3
  • Alloying elements and their effects on steel
  • Stainless steels & tool steels
  • Aluminium alloys & precipitation hardening
  • Copper alloys (brass, bronze)
  • Superalloys (basic idea & applications)

Unit 4
  • Polymers (types + important properties)
  • Engineering plastics (PVC, PTFE, Nylon basics)
  • Ceramics (Al₂O₃, SiC – properties)
  • Composites (matrix & reinforcement concept)

Unit 5
  • Plastic deformation (slip & twinning)
  • Types of fracture (ductile & brittle)
  • Hardness tests (Brinell, Rockwell, Vickers)
  • Impact test (Izod & Charpy)
  • Fatigue & creep (basic mechanisms)

🔥 Ultra Important (Must Focus)
If time is less, study:
  • Iron–Iron carbide diagram
  • Heat treatment + TTT diagram
  • Alloy steels & aluminium alloys
  • Composites & polymers basics
  • Hardness + impact tests
