About ME3592 Metrology and Measurements

ME3592 Metrology and Measurements covers the principles and techniques of measurement in engineering. The subject introduces students to measurement errors, calibration, linear and angular measurement, and advanced metrology systems.

Students learn about limits, fits, tolerances, GD&T, surface finish, and coordinate measuring machines. The course emphasizes accuracy, uncertainty, and the application of modern measurement technologies in manufacturing and quality control.

Mastery of these topics is essential for careers in quality assurance, manufacturing, and research, enabling students to ensure precision and reliability in engineering processes.

Important Topics

ME3592 Metrology and Measurements, unit-wise:

Unit 1
  • Measurement basics & errors (types, causes, control)
  • Measurement uncertainty & basic estimation
  • Statistical analysis of measurement data
  • Calibration of instruments
  • Principle of air gauging

Unit 2
  • Linear measurement instruments (vernier, micrometer, comparators)
  • Angular measurement (sine bar, autocollimator)
  • Gear measurement (pitch, runout, profile)
  • Screw thread measurement (pitch, lead, pitch diameter)

Unit 3
  • Limits, fits & tolerances (basic concepts & problems)
  • Interchangeability & selective assembly
  • Limit gauge design
  • Process capability & tolerance stack-up

Unit 4
  • GD&T basics (datums, geometric tolerances)
  • Measurement of surface roughness (parameters & methods)
  • Surface finish measurement techniques

Unit 5
  • Coordinate Measuring Machine (CMM – types & applications)
  • Laser-based measurements (interferometer basics)
  • Machine vision systems (basic concept & applications)
  • Computer-aided metrology
