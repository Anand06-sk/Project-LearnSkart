About EE3591 Power Electronics

EE3591 Power Electronics is a key Semester 5 subject for Anna University Electrical and Electronics Engineering students. This course covers the operation, characteristics, and applications of power semiconductor devices, converters, inverters, and controllers. These EE3591 notes provide a strong foundation in switching devices, rectifiers, inverters, and AC/DC control techniques.

With our Anna University study materials and EE3591 important topics, you will master MOSFET and IGBT switching, PWM techniques, rectifier and inverter analysis, and AC controller operation. The content is designed for both theoretical clarity and practical application.

These EE3591 Power Electronics notes help you analyze converter circuits, solve numerical problems, and prepare thoroughly for university exams and interviews.

Important Topics

EE3591 Power Electronics – Important Topics
UNIT 1
  • MOSFET switching characteristics (dynamic behavior)
  • Driver & snubber circuits
  • DC-DC converters: Buck, Boost, Buck-Boost
  • Switching losses & basic thermal considerations
UNIT 2
  • IGBT characteristics (static & switching)
  • Single phase & three phase VSI
  • PWM techniques (Sinusoidal, Space Vector)
  • Voltage control of inverters
  • Harmonics in inverters & reduction methods
UNIT 3
  • Single phase rectifiers (half wave, full wave, bridge)
  • Three phase diode bridge rectifier
  • Filters (capacitor, LC)
  • Performance factors (ripple, distortion)
UNIT 4
  • SCR operation & characteristics
  • Controlled rectifiers (1-phase & 3-phase)
  • Firing angle control
  • Effect of source inductance
  • Power factor & harmonics in converters
UNIT 5
  • TRIAC operation & triggering
  • AC phase control methods
  • Single phase & three phase AC controllers
  • Output voltage control techniques
