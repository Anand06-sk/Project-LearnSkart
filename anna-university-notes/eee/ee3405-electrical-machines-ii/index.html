About EE3405 Electrical Machines II

EE3405 Electrical Machines II is a core Semester 4 subject for Anna University Electrical and Electronics Engineering students. This course focuses on the construction, operation, and analysis of synchronous machines and induction motors. These EE3405 notes provide a comprehensive understanding of alternators, synchronous motors, three-phase and single-phase induction motors, and their industrial applications.

With our Anna University study materials and EE3405 important topics, you will master phasor diagrams, voltage regulation, parallel operation, torque-slip characteristics, and advanced starting methods. The content is designed for both theoretical clarity and practical problem-solving.

These EE3405 Electrical Machines II notes help you analyze machine performance, solve numerical problems, and prepare confidently for university exams and interviews.

Important Topics

EE3405 Electrical Machines II – Important Topics
UNIT 1
  • Construction & working of alternator
  • EMF equation of alternator
  • Voltage regulation (EMF method)
  • Parallel operation of alternators
  • Phasor diagrams of alternator
UNIT 2
  • Synchronous motor construction & working
  • Equivalent circuit of synchronous motor
  • Power factor control & phasor diagrams
  • Starting methods of synchronous motor
  • V and inverted V curves
UNIT 3
  • Three phase induction motor construction & working
  • Torque-slip characteristics
  • Maximum torque condition
  • Circle diagram of induction motor
  • Power stages & losses in induction motor
UNIT 4
  • Starting methods of induction motor
  • Rotor resistance starter
  • Slip power recovery methods (Kramer & Scherbius)
  • Cascade operation of induction motor
  • Static drives (Kramer & Scherbius systems)
UNIT 5
  • Single phase induction motor basics
  • Double revolving field theory
  • Types of single phase motors
  • Hysteresis motor & AC series motor
  • Repulsion motor & servo motor
  • Linear induction motor
