Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Why is Hamming code important in digital systems?
Hamming code enables automatic error detection and correction in digital data transmission, critical for reliable communication in noisy environments. Single-bit errors can be detected and corrected without data retransmission.

What are the advantages of CMOS over TTL logic?
CMOS offers lower power consumption, higher noise immunity, better integration density, and wider supply voltage tolerance compared to TTL, making it preferred for modern microelectronics and battery-powered applications.

How do flip-flops form the basis of sequential logic?
Flip-flops store binary state and respond to clock signals, enabling sequential circuit behavior. Master-slave configuration prevents timing hazards, making them essential building blocks for counters and state machines.

What is the significance of hazards in asynchronous circuits?
Hazards cause unwanted transients in circuit output due to propagation delays. Understanding static and dynamic hazards enables designers to eliminate glitches and ensure reliable operation in asynchronous sequential circuits.

How do PLDs simplify digital circuit design?
Programmable Logic Devices (PAL, PLA) replace multiple discrete logic gates with configurable devices, reducing circuit complexity, board space, and design time while improving reliability and flexibility for system modifications.

What advantages does VHDL provide for digital design?
VHDL enables hardware description at multiple abstraction levels, supports simulation before hardware implementation, facilitates reusability through libraries, and simplifies design documentation and team collaboration in complex digital systems.
