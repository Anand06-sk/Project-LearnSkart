About EE3402 Linear Integrated Circuits

EE3402 Linear Integrated Circuits is a core Semester 4 subject for Anna University Electrical and Electronics Engineering students. This course explores the design, fabrication, and application of integrated circuits, focusing on operational amplifiers, filters, voltage regulators, and analog/digital conversion techniques. These EE3402 notes help you understand the principles of IC fabrication, amplifier configurations, and the use of analog building blocks in real-world circuits.

With our Anna University study materials and EE3402 important topics, you will master the analysis and design of op-amp circuits, active filters, oscillators, and power supply systems. These resources are tailored for exam preparation and practical engineering applications, making complex concepts easy to grasp.

Using these EE3402 Linear Integrated Circuits notes, you can confidently solve circuit problems, design analog systems, and prepare for university exams with clarity and precision.

Important Topics

EE3402 Linear Integrated Circuits – Important Topics
UNIT 1
  • CMOS fabrication (N-well, P-well)
  • Epitaxy, Masking, Etching, Diffusion, Metallization
  • Ion implantation
  • IC classifications
  • IC packaging techniques
  • Diode & capacitor fabrication
UNIT 2
  • Inverting & Non-inverting amplifiers
  • Differentiator & Integrator
  • Op-Amp stability methods
  • AC characteristics of Op-Amp
  • Voltage ↔ Current converters
  • Adder & Subtractor circuits
UNIT 3
  • Active filters (Low-pass Butterworth)
  • Log & Antilog amplifiers
  • Clippers & Clampers
  • Sample and Hold circuit
  • Weighted resistor DAC
  • R-2R ladder DAC
UNIT 4
  • Phase Locked Loop (PLL)
  • Voltage Controlled Oscillator (VCO)
  • 555 Timer (Astable & Monostable)
  • PLL calculations
UNIT 5
  • Voltage regulators
  • IC 723 regulator
  • Switched Mode Power Supply (SMPS)
  • Instrumentation amplifier (AD623)
  • Applications of regulators
