About EE3503 Control Systems

EE3503 Control Systems is a core Semester 5 subject for Anna University Electrical and Electronics Engineering students. This course covers the modeling, analysis, and design of control systems for electrical, mechanical, and industrial applications. These EE3503 notes provide a strong foundation in transfer functions, stability analysis, frequency response, and state space methods.

With our Anna University study materials and EE3503 important topics, you will master block diagram reduction, root locus, Bode and Nyquist plots, compensator design, and PID controllers. The content is designed for both theoretical understanding and practical problem-solving.

These EE3503 Control Systems notes help you analyze system stability, design controllers, and prepare effectively for university exams and engineering interviews.

Important Topics

EE3503 Control Systems – Important Topics
UNIT 1
  • Open loop vs Closed loop systems
  • Transfer function models (electrical & mechanical systems)
  • Block diagram reduction
  • Signal flow graph & Mason’s gain formula
UNIT 2
  • Time response (first & second order systems)
  • Time domain specifications
  • Routh-Hurwitz stability criterion
  • Root locus (construction & rules)
  • Effect of poles and zeros
UNIT 3
  • Bode plot
  • Nyquist plot
  • Frequency response analysis
  • Gain margin & phase margin
  • Lead and lag compensators (concept)
UNIT 4
  • State space representation
  • State transition matrix
  • Eigenvalues & system stability
  • Controllability & observability
UNIT 5
  • Design of compensators (lead, lag, lag-lead)
  • PID controller basics
  • Ziegler-Nichols tuning method
  • Control system design using root locus & Bode
