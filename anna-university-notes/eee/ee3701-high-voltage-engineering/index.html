About High Voltage Engineering (EE3701)

EE3701 High Voltage Engineering is a specialized subject for Anna University Semester 7 EEE students. This course explores the generation, measurement, and application of high voltages in modern power systems and industries. These EE3701 notes help you understand the behavior of electrical insulation, breakdown mechanisms, and the effects of overvoltages on equipment.

With our Anna University study materials and EE3701 important topics, you will learn about insulation coordination, dielectric properties, high voltage testing, and advanced electrostatic applications. The content is designed to build both theoretical knowledge and practical skills for real-world engineering challenges.

These High Voltage Engineering notes are ideal for exam preparation, project work, and interviews, providing a strong foundation for careers in power systems, testing labs, and electrical equipment design.

Unit 1
  • Types of overvoltages – lightning, switching, temporary
  • Causes & effects of overvoltages on power systems

  • Insulation coordination (basic concept & importance)

  • Properties of dielectric materials
  • Breakdown in gases
  • Uniform field
