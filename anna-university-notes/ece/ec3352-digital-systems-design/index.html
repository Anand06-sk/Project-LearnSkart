About EC3352 Digital Systems Design

EC3352 is a core subject for Anna University Semester 3 students, focusing on the principles and design of digital systems. The subject covers Boolean algebra, combinational and sequential circuits, logic families, and programmable devices. EC3352 Important Topics (Unit-wise)

These are the most important unit-wise topics for EC3352 (Digital Systems Design - R2021). Practice these topics regularly to strengthen core concepts and improve exam performance.

Unit I – Basic Concepts

  • Boolean algebra simplification (Sum of Products and Product of Sums)
  • Karnaugh Map (K-Map) simplification (including don’t care conditions)
  • Canonical forms (Minterm and Maxterm representation)
  • Implementation using universal gates (NAND and NOR)
  • Tabulation method (Quine–McCluskey method)
  • Number system conversions and representations

Unit II – Combinational Logic Circuits

  • Design of combinational circuits (step-by-step problem solving)
  • Adders: Half Adder, Full Adder, Carry Look Ahead Adder
  • Multiplexer and Demultiplexer (design and applications)
  • Encoder, Decoder and Priority Encoder
  • Magnitude Comparator
  • Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU – basic design concepts)
  • Code converters and BCD Adder
  • Parity generator and checker

Unit III – Synchronous Sequential Circuits

  • Flip-Flops (SR, JK, D, T) and their conversions
  • Design of sequential circuits using Moore and Mealy models
  • State diagram, state table and state minimization
  • Counters (Ripple, Synchronous, Ring counters)
  • Shift Registers (including Universal Shift Register)
  • Clocking and triggering methods of Flip-Flops

Unit IV – Asynchronous Sequential Circuits

  • Race conditions and race-free state assignment
  • Hazards (Static, Dynamic, Essential hazards)
  • Design of hazard-free circuits
  • Stable and unstable states
  • Fundamental mode and pulse mode circuits

Unit V – Logic Families and Programmable Logic Devices

  • Characteristics of logic families (TTL, CMOS, ECL comparison)
  • Propagation delay, Fan-in, Fan-out, Noise margin
  • Programmable Logic Devices (PROM, PLA, PAL)
  • Memory devices (ROM, EPROM, EEPROM)
  • Implementation of logic circuits using standard ICs
Practice these questions regularly and revise unit by unit to build confidence for university exams.
