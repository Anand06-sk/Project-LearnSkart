About EC3251 Circuit Analysis

EC3251 is a core subject for Anna University Semester 2 students, focusing on the analysis of electrical circuits. The subject covers DC and AC circuit analysis, network theorems, resonance, transients, and network topology. EC3251 Important Topics (Unit-wise)

These are the most important unit-wise topics for EC3251 (Circuit Analysis - R2021). Practice these topics regularly to strengthen core concepts and improve exam performance.

Unit I – Basic Circuit Analysis

  • Series and parallel circuits (current, voltage, power calculations)
  • Equivalent resistance and voltage division
  • Nodal analysis (voltage calculation)
  • Mesh analysis (current calculation)
  • Basic DC circuit problem solving

Unit II – Network Theorems

  • Thevenin’s theorem and applications
  • Norton’s theorem and equivalent circuits
  • Maximum power transfer theorem
  • Dual networks
  • Wheatstone bridge analysis

Unit III – AC Circuits

  • RL, RC, RLC circuits (analysis and calculations)
  • Phasor diagrams (voltage and current relationships)
  • Impedance and admittance calculations
  • Power factor and power calculations
  • Sinusoidal waveform analysis (RMS, average, frequency)

Unit IV – Resonance and Transients

  • Series and parallel resonance
  • Resonant frequency and Q-factor
  • Bandwidth calculation
  • Voltage and current at resonance
  • Transient response of RC and RL circuits

Unit V – Coupled Circuits & Network Topology

  • Mutual inductance and coefficient of coupling
  • Series aiding and opposing connections
  • Linear transformer basics
  • Network topology (graph, incidence matrix)
  • Tree, tie-set and network analysis
Practice these questions regularly and revise unit by unit to build confidence for university exams.
