EC3551 Important Topics (Unit-wise)

These are the most important unit-wise topics for EC3551 (Transmission Lines and RF Systems - R2021). Practice these topics regularly to strengthen core concepts and improve exam performance.

Unit I – Transmission Line Fundamentals

  • Explain the general solution of transmission line equations and analyze wave propagation
  • Derive and explain the condition for a distortionless transmission line
  • Explain reflection coefficient and calculate voltage, current, and power on a transmission line
  • Analyze open and short circuited transmission lines with input impedance derivation
  • Explain loading methods in transmission lines and their significance

Unit II – Standing Waves and High Frequency Transmission Lines

  • Derive the transmission line equations at high frequencies
  • Explain standing waves, nodes, and antinodes with VSWR derivation
  • Derive input impedance of a lossless transmission line
  • Explain methods to measure VSWR and wavelength in transmission lines
  • Analyze reflection losses and power measurement in RF lines

Unit III – Impedance Matching and Smith Chart

  • Explain impedance matching using quarter wave transformer with derivation
  • Discuss stub matching techniques (single and double stub)
  • Explain the Smith Chart and its applications in solving transmission line problems
  • Solve impedance matching problems using Smith Chart method
  • Compare quarter wave, half wave, and eighth wave matching techniques

Unit IV – Waveguide Propagation

  • Explain propagation of TE, TM, and TEM waves between parallel conducting plates
  • Derive and explain TE and TM modes in rectangular waveguides
  • Explain wave propagation in circular waveguides
  • Compare characteristics of TE, TM, and TEM waves

Unit V – RF Components and Systems

  • Explain different RF active components: BJT, FET, HEMT
  • Discuss basics of MMIC (Monolithic Microwave Integrated Circuits)
  • Explain design and working of RF filters, couplers, and power dividers
  • Analyze low noise amplifiers (LNA) and power amplifiers
  • Explain power relations in RF amplifiers
