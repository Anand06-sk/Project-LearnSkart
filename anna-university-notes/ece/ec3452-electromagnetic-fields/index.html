EC3452 Electromagnetic Fields is a core subject for Anna University Semester 4 ECE students. EC3452 Important Topics (Unit-wise)

These are the most important unit-wise topics for EC3452 (Electromagnetic Fields - R2021). Practice these topics regularly to strengthen core concepts and improve exam performance.

UNIT I – Vector Calculus & Coordinate Systems

  • Gradient, Divergence and Curl (definition + problems)
  • Coordinate transformations (Cartesian ↔ Cylindrical ↔ Spherical)
  • Curl of vector fields in cylindrical coordinates
  • Evaluation of scalar and vector field problems

UNIT II – Electrostatics

  • Gauss’s Law and its applications (sphere, capacitor problems)
  • Electric flux density and charge density (numericals)
  • Derivation of Laplace and Poisson equations
  • Capacitance of parallel plate capacitor (derivation + problems)
  • Electric field from potential (E = −∇V)

UNIT III – Magnetostatics

  • Ampere’s Circuital Law (applications + derivations)
  • Magnetic field intensity (H) for standard configurations
  • Infinite line current
  • Current sheet
  • Coaxial cable
  • Magnetic field due to circular loop and straight conductor

UNIT IV – Time Varying Fields

  • Maxwell’s Equations (integral and point form)
  • Displacement current and modified Ampere’s law
  • Wave equation derivation for Electric and Magnetic fields
  • Uniform plane wave properties

UNIT V – Electromagnetic Waves

  • Boundary conditions for Electric and Magnetic fields
  • Poynting vector (derivation + physical meaning)
  • Plane wave propagation in lossless medium (numericals)
  • Reflection of plane waves (normal incidence)
Practice these questions regularly and revise unit by unit to build confidence for university exams.
