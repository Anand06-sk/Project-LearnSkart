About EC3451 Linear Integrated Circuits

EC3451 Linear Integrated Circuits is a core subject for Anna University Semester 4 ECE students. This subject covers the fundamentals and applications of operational amplifiers, phase locked loops, data converters, voltage regulators, and waveform generators. EC3451 Important Topics (Unit-wise)

These are the most important unit-wise topics for EC3451 (Linear Integrated Circuits - R2021). Practice these topics regularly to strengthen core concepts and improve exam performance.

UNIT I – Operational Amplifier Basics

  • Operational amplifier equivalent circuit and working
  • Operational amplifier characteristics (Direct Current and Alternating Current parameters)
  • Differential amplifier with active load (working)
  • LF155 operational amplifier basics

UNIT II – Applications of Operational Amplifier

  • Clipper and clamper circuits using operational amplifier
  • Instrumentation amplifier (circuit + gain derivation)
  • First order and second order Butterworth filters (Low pass and High pass)
  • Design of second order Low pass Butterworth filter

UNIT III – Phase Locked Loop and Applications

  • Phase Locked Loop (PLL) basics and IC565
  • Voltage Controlled Oscillator (VCO – working + expression for frequency)
  • Frequency synthesizer using PLL
  • Analog multiplier (working + application)
  • AM and FM detection using PLL

UNIT IV – Data Converters

  • Digital to Analog Converter (DAC – Binary weighted resistor)
  • Analog to Digital Converter (ADC – Dual slope method)
  • Successive approximation ADC
  • Voltage to time conversion ADC

UNIT V – Regulators and Waveform Generators

  • Voltage regulators (fixed and adjustable)
  • IC 723 voltage regulator (block diagram + working)
  • Monostable multivibrator (circuit + time period derivation)
  • Voltage to Frequency and Frequency to Voltage converters
Practice these questions regularly and revise unit by unit to build confidence for university exams.
