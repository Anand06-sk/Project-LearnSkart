EC3491 Important Topics (Unit-wise)

These are the most important unit-wise topics for EC3491 (Communication Systems - R2021). Practice these topics regularly to strengthen core concepts and improve exam performance.

Unit I – Analog Modulation Techniques

  • Square law modulator and detector (working + diagram)
  • Superheterodyne receiver (block diagram + advantages)
  • Hilbert transform (properties + applications)
  • DSB-SC and SSB-SC modulation (derivations)
  • Pre-envelope and complex envelope concepts

Unit II – Noise and Pulse Modulation

  • Signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) for Amplitude Modulation receiver
  • Figure of merit (comparison: Amplitude Modulation, DSB-SC, SSB)
  • Pulse modulation techniques (Pulse Amplitude Modulation, Pulse Width Modulation, Pulse Position Modulation – generation + comparison)
  • Quantization (levels, error, resolution problems)
  • Time Division Multiplexing vs Frequency Division Multiplexing

Unit III – Digital Transmission Techniques

  • Delta Modulation (quantization noise + Signal-to-noise ratio derivation)
  • Receiver noise analysis
  • Cyclic codes (encoding and decoding concepts)

Unit IV – Digital Modulation

  • Bit Error Rate derivation for Binary Phase Shift Keying
  • Bit Error Rate derivation for Quadrature Phase Shift Keying
  • Binary Frequency Shift Keying vs Binary Phase Shift Keying (comparison)
  • Performance comparison (Power Spectral Density and Bit Error Rate)

Unit V – Advanced Digital Communication

  • Nyquist criterion for zero Inter Symbol Interference (derivation)
  • Channel equalization (Zero Forcing Equalizer and Minimum Mean Square Error Equalizer)
  • Matched filter (working + derivation)
  • Probability of error and receiver noise concepts
  • Bandwidth efficient modulation techniques
Practice these questions regularly and revise unit by unit to build confidence for university exams.
