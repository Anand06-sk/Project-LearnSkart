About EC3353 Electronic Devices and Circuits

EC3353 is a core subject for Anna University Semester 3 students, focusing on the principles and applications of electronic devices and circuits. The subject covers semiconductor devices, amplifiers, oscillators, power amplifiers, and converters. These EC3353 notes are designed to help you master device operation, circuit analysis, and design for both internal assessments and university exams.

EC3353 Important Topics (Unit-wise)

These are the most important unit-wise topics for EC3353 (Electronic Devices and Circuits - R2021). Practice these topics regularly to strengthen core concepts and improve exam performance.

Unit I – Semiconductor Devices

  • Zener diode as voltage regulator (design + working)
  • Half wave and full wave rectifier (derivations + comparison)
  • BJT characteristics and configurations (CE focus)
  • MOSFET characteristics and operation

Unit II – Amplifiers

  • Load line and operating point (Q-point analysis)
  • Biasing methods (BJT focus)
  • CE amplifier analysis (gain + frequency response)
  • MOSFET CS amplifier (gain + basics)

Unit III – Multistage & Differential Amplifiers

  • Differential amplifier (common mode & differential mode)
  • Cascode amplifier (working + advantages)
  • Multistage amplifier gain and frequency response

Unit IV – Feedback & Oscillators

  • Negative feedback (types + advantages)
  • Condition for oscillation (Barkhausen criterion)
  • RC Phase shift oscillator
  • Wien bridge oscillator

Unit V – Power Amplifiers & Converters

  • Power amplifier classes (Class A, B, AB comparison)
  • Class AB amplifier (important)
  • DC-DC converters (Buck, Boost, Buck-Boost basics + working)
Practice these questions regularly and revise unit by unit to build confidence for university exams.
