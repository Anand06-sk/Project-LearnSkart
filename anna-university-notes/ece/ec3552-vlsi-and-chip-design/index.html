EC3552 Important Topics (Unit-wise)

These are the most important unit-wise topics for EC3552 (VLSI and Chip Design - R2021). Practice these topics regularly to strengthen core concepts and improve exam performance.

Unit I – MOS Transistor and CMOS Logic

  • Explain MOS transistor I–V characteristics (ideal vs non-ideal) and analyze regions of operation
  • Compare NMOS and CMOS logic families with advantages and limitations
  • Explain CMOS inverter operation under static and dynamic conditions
  • Discuss technology scaling and its impact on power consumption and performance
  • Analyze different components of power dissipation in CMOS circuits

Unit II – Circuit Design and Power Optimization

  • Explain propagation delay in CMOS circuits and factors affecting it
  • Draw and explain stick diagrams and layout diagrams for basic logic gates
  • Compare static logic and dynamic logic circuits
  • Explain pass transistor logic and its advantages
  • Discuss low power design techniques in VLSI

Unit III – Sequential Logic and Timing

  • Explain static and dynamic latches and registers with working
  • Discuss pipelining in VLSI design and its performance improvement
  • Explain clocking strategies and timing issues in synchronous systems
  • Compare synchronous and self-timed circuit design
  • Analyze timing classification of digital systems

Unit IV – Memory and Arithmetic Circuits

  • Explain interconnect effects (R, L, C) and their impact on VLSI circuits
  • Discuss memory architecture and its building blocks
  • Explain design of adders and multipliers in VLSI systems
  • Compare ROM, PLA, and FPGA implementations
  • Analyze wire models and delay in interconnects

Unit V – ASIC Design and Testing

  • Explain ASIC design flow from specification to fabrication
  • Discuss testing and verification techniques in VLSI design
  • Explain fault models and automatic test pattern generation (ATPG)
  • Describe Design for Testability (DFT) and scan design techniques
  • Explain basics of Verilog test benches for chip verification
