About BE3254 Electrical and Instrumentation Engineering

BE3254 introduces the fundamental concepts of electrical machines, transformers, AC and DC motors, power systems, and instrumentation. The subject covers the construction, working principles, and applications of various electrical devices and measurement systems. It is essential for understanding how electrical energy is generated, transformed, measured, and protected in engineering practice. These notes are designed to help you master the core topics and perform well in both internal assessments and university exams.

BE3254 Important Topics (Unit-wise)

UNIT I – TRANSFORMERS

  • Phasor diagram of single-phase transformer (especially capacitive load)
  • EMF equation of transformer (numerical problems)
  • Construction and working principle of single-phase transformer
  • Equivalent circuit of transformer
  • Voltage regulation of transformer
  • Harmonics in transformer (causes and reduction methods)
  • Autotransformer (working and comparison with two-winding transformer)

UNIT II – DC MACHINES & STEPPER MOTOR

  • Construction and working of DC generator
  • EMF equation of DC generator
  • EMF and torque equation of DC motor
  • Characteristics of DC motor
  • Stepper motor (working, timing diagram, applications)
  • Brushless DC motor (construction and characteristics)

UNIT III – AC MACHINES

  • Three-phase induction motor: Construction, working, equivalent circuit
  • Speed control methods
  • Single-phase induction motor: Working principle and characteristics
  • Alternator: Construction and working
  • Synchronous motor: Working principle, Torque equation

UNIT IV – MEASUREMENTS & INSTRUMENTATION

  • Moving coil vs moving iron instruments (comparison)
  • Functional elements of measurement system (block diagram)
  • Digital Storage Oscilloscope (block diagram and working)
  • Measurement of three-phase power
  • Energy meter (construction and working)
  • General instrumentation system (elements and functions)

UNIT V – POWER SYSTEM & PROTECTION

  • Power system structure: Generation, transmission, distribution
  • Earthing methods (any one in detail)
  • Protective devices: Fuse (selection of rating)
  • Circuit breakers (types and working)
  • Earth leakage circuit breaker (ELCB)

Exam Focus Strategy:

  1. First priority: Derivations and diagrams (transformer, motors, DSO, energy meter)
  2. Second priority: Working principles + construction
  3. Third priority: Comparisons and short notes
  4. Fourth priority: Numerical problems (EMF, torque, transformer calculations)
