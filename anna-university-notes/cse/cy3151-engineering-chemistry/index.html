CY3151 - Engineering Chemistry Important Questions (Unit-wise)

Engineering Chemistry helps students understand practical scientific concepts used in real engineering applications. Unit-wise preparation makes it easier to revise theory, process-based answers, and diagram-oriented questions for exams.

UNIT I - Water Chemistry & Treatment

This unit explains water quality, treatment techniques, and industrial water management methods. It is important for learning process-based questions on purification and treatment systems.

  • Breakpoint chlorination - Definition & process
  • Reverse osmosis (RO) vs. Demineralization - Differences & principle
  • Boiler water treatment - Scale & sludge formation, internal conditioning methods
  • Ion-exchange method - Working principle with diagram
  • Desalination techniques - RO & distillation

UNIT II - Nanotechnology & Applications

This unit focuses on nanomaterials, preparation techniques, and real-world applications. It combines conceptual theory with process-based questions commonly asked in exams.

  • Nanomaterials vs. Bulk materials - Differences
  • Carbon nanotubes (CNTs) - Types & applications
  • Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) & Electrospinning - Working principle
  • Nanoclusters & Nanowires - Properties & applications
  • Nanomaterials in medicine & electronics - Examples

UNIT III - Phase Rule & Composite Materials

This unit covers phase diagrams and advanced materials used in engineering design. Students should practice diagram-based explanation questions carefully in this unit.

  • One-component system - Definition & example
  • Eutectic phase diagram - Construction & explanation
  • Metal matrix & Polymer matrix composites - Properties & uses
  • Hybrid composites - Definition & significance
  • Lead-Silver phase diagram - Explanation

UNIT IV - Fuels & Energy Sources

This unit introduces industrial fuel processing and energy conversion methods. It includes process-oriented questions that are frequently asked in university exams.

  • Bergius process for synthetic petrol - Steps & catalyst importance
  • Otto Hoffmann process - Metallurgical coke manufacture
  • Flue gas analysis (Orsat method) - Working principle

UNIT V - Electrochemical Energy Storage

This unit focuses on electrochemistry and modern energy storage systems. It includes reaction-based, mechanism-based, and application-based questions useful for scoring answers.

  • Lithium-ion battery - Working, charging/discharging reactions
  • Lead-acid battery - Working principle & reactions
  • Microbial fuel cells (MFCs) - Mechanism & applications
  • Hydrogen-Oxygen fuel cell - Diagram & working principle
  • Solar cells & Wind energy - Working mechanism

Most Repeated Questions (Must Study)

These questions are frequently repeated and should be given special priority during final revision.

  1. Explain breakpoint chlorination and municipal water treatment.
  2. Describe the ion-exchange process with a neat diagram.
  3. Explain CVD & Electrospinning methods for nanomaterial preparation.
  4. Discuss the Bergius process for synthetic petrol production.
  5. Explain the Otto Hoffmann method for metallurgical coke.
  6. Describe the working of a lithium-ion battery with reactions.
  7. Construct a simple eutectic phase diagram and explain it.
  8. Explain the working principle of microbial fuel cells.

Exam Preparation Tips

  • Focus on key concepts like water treatment, nanotechnology, phase diagrams, fuels, and electrochemistry.
  • Practice process-based and diagram-based answers with clear stepwise explanations.
  • Revise one unit per day and keep separate short notes for definitions and mechanisms.
  • Prioritize the Most Repeated Questions section during last-minute revision.

CY3151 - ENGINEERING CHEMISTRY

SYLLABUS

UNIT I WATER AND ITS TREATMENT 9

Water: Sources and impurities, Water quality parameters: Definition and significance of-color, odour, turbidity, pH, hardness, alkalinity, TDS, COD and BOD, fluoride and arsenic. Municipal water treatment: primary treatment and disinfection (UV, Ozonation, break-point chlorination). Desalination of brackish water: Reverse Osmosis. Boiler troubles: Scale and sludge, Boiler corrosion, Caustic embrittlement, Priming & foaming. Treatment of boiler feed water: Internal treatment (phosphate, colloidal, sodium aluminate and calgon conditioning) and External treatment - Ion exchange demineralization and zeolite process.

UNIT II NANOCHEMISTRY 9

Basics: Distinction between molecules, nanomaterials and bulk materials; Size-dependent properties (optical, electrical, mechanical and magnetic); Types of nanomaterials: Definition, properties and uses of - nanoparticle, nanocluster, nanorod, nanowire and nanotube. Preparation of nanomaterials: sol-gel, solvothermal, laser ablation, chemical vapour deposition, electrochemical deposition and electro spinning. Applications of nanomaterials in medicine, agriculture, energy, electronics and catalysis.

UNIT III PHASE RULE AND COMPOSITES 9

Phase rule: Introduction, definition of terms with examples. One component system - water system; Reduced phase rule; Construction of a simple eutectic phase diagram - Thermal analysis; Two component system: lead-silver system - Pattinson process. Composites: Introduction: Definition & Need for composites; Constitution: Matrix materials (Polymer matrix, metal matrix and ceramic matrix) and Reinforcement (fiber, particulates, flakes and whiskers). Properties and applications of: Metal matrix composites (MMC), Ceramic matrix composites and Polymer matrix composites. Hybrid composites - definition and examples.

UNIT IV FUELS AND COMBUSTION 9

Fuels: Introduction: Classification of fuels; Coal and coke: Analysis of coal (proximate and ultimate),Carbonization, Manufacture of metallurgical coke (Otto Hoffmann method). Petroleum and Diesel: Manufacture of synthetic petrol (Bergius process), Knocking - octane number, diesel oil - cetane number; Power alcohol and biodiesel. Combustion of fuels: Introduction: Calorific value - higher and lower calorific values, Theoretical calculation of calorific value; Ignition temperature: spontaneous ignition temperature, Explosive range; Flue gas analysis - ORSAT Method. CO2 emission and carbon footprint.

UNIT V ENERGY SOURCES AND STORAGE DEVICES 9

Stability of nucleus: mass defect (problems), binding energy; Nuclear energy: light water nuclear power plant, breeder reactor. Solar energy conversion: Principle, working and applications of solar cells; Recent developments in solar cell materials. Wind energy; Geothermal energy; Batteries:Types of batteries, Primary battery - dry cell, Secondary battery - lead acid battery and lithium-ion-battery; Electric vehicles - working principles; Fuel cells: H2-O2 fuel cell, microbial fuel cell;Supercapacitors: Storage principle, types and examples.

TOTAL: 45 PERIODS
