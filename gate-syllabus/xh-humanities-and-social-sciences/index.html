GATE 2026 Humanities and Social Sciences Syllabus and Complete Preparation Guide

GATE Humanities and Social Sciences (XH) Syllabus 2026 PDF

Subject Code: XH

Total Marks: 100 | Duration: 3 Hours

Question Pattern: MCQ, MSQ, and NAT are used across the paper; the exact count can vary by year.

GATE 2026 is a national level examination that evaluates analytical ability and subject knowledge at the undergraduate level. The organizing institute for GATE 2026 is the Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati. A valid GATE score is accepted for postgraduate admissions and also used by several organizations for recruitment.

The Humanities and Social Sciences paper, code XH, combines a common reasoning and comprehension section with a discipline specific section. It is designed for candidates from humanities, social sciences, and interdisciplinary backgrounds who want to pursue higher studies or research in their domain.

About the XH Paper

XH is an interdisciplinary paper that tests both reasoning skills and subject depth. It allows candidates to choose an optional discipline aligned with their academic background. This structure rewards candidates who combine critical reading skills with strong conceptual clarity.

The paper is well suited for candidates in economics, English, linguistics, philosophy, psychology, and sociology. Students from related fields can also attempt XH if they are confident in one optional area and willing to build reasoning ability through practice.

GATE 2026 Exam Pattern

The exam is conducted in computer based mode for three hours and carries 100 marks. General Aptitude contributes 15 marks. The remaining marks are divided between a compulsory section on reasoning and comprehension and the chosen optional discipline section.

  • General Aptitude tests verbal and analytical ability
  • Section A is compulsory and covers reasoning and comprehension
  • One optional discipline is chosen as per the official syllabus
  • Negative marking applies only to MCQ type questions

Eligibility

Candidates in the third year or higher of undergraduate degree programs are eligible to apply. Graduates and students in integrated or postgraduate programs can also appear. There is no age limit for the GATE exam.

XH is a good choice for students in humanities and social science programs, as well as candidates in interdisciplinary areas such as public policy, development studies, or communication, provided they choose a suitable optional discipline.

Detailed Syllabus Overview

Section A: Reasoning and Comprehension

The compulsory section includes reading comprehension, logical reasoning, basic verbal ability, and analytical interpretation. Questions may involve passages, argument structure, inference, and basic quantitative reasoning. Consistent practice helps improve speed and accuracy in this section.

Optional Discipline Sections

XH allows candidates to choose one optional discipline as per the official syllabus. Common options include economics, English, linguistics, philosophy, psychology, and sociology. Candidates should verify the official syllabus PDF for the exact list and detailed topics.

Economics

Economics covers microeconomics, macroeconomics, mathematical economics, econometrics basics, and Indian economy topics. Candidates should focus on demand and supply analysis, market structures, national income, monetary policy, and basic optimization concepts.

English

English includes language and communication, literature, and critical analysis. Emphasis is placed on reading, comprehension, grammar, and interpretive skills. Candidates should practice analyzing texts and understanding rhetorical strategies.

Linguistics

Linguistics covers phonetics, phonology, morphology, syntax, semantics, and sociolinguistics. It requires clarity in linguistic terminology and the ability to analyze linguistic data and structures.

Philosophy

Philosophy includes logic, epistemology, ethics, and metaphysics, as well as themes from Indian and Western philosophy. Questions often assess conceptual clarity and the ability to distinguish between philosophical arguments.

Psychology

Psychology covers cognitive, developmental, social, and biological psychology, along with research methods and statistics. The emphasis is on understanding key theories, experiments, and research designs.

Sociology

Sociology includes sociological theory, social institutions, stratification, research methods, and contemporary social issues. Candidates should be comfortable with core theorists and applied sociological analysis.

Importance of Previous Year Questions

PYQs show the balance between Section A and the optional discipline. They reveal typical question formats, the depth of conceptual testing, and the amount of data interpretation required. Solving PYQs also helps candidates identify commonly repeated themes.

Practice with PYQs builds familiarity with the reading style used in comprehension questions and helps in refining optional subject preparation.

Subject Analysis and Scoring Strategy

Section A is scoring for candidates who practice regularly, as it rewards careful reading and logic. Optional sections require deeper conceptual clarity and are best approached by building strong notes and revising key themes.

In MCQs, avoid guessing. Use MSQ and NAT questions to demonstrate understanding without negative marking. Focus on accuracy rather than attempting every question.

Common Pitfalls

Common errors include misreading passage questions, confusing similar theoretical terms, and neglecting research method basics. Regular revision and note consolidation reduce these mistakes.

Preparation Strategy

Begin by building a solid base in the chosen optional discipline, then add daily practice for reasoning and comprehension. Use standard textbooks or lecture notes for theory and create concise summary notes for key concepts.

For Section A, practice passages and logical reasoning sets under timed conditions. For the optional discipline, solve topic wise questions and create mind maps to connect theories and concepts.

Suggested Study Plan

In the first phase, focus on understanding the optional discipline and building a glossary of key terms. In the second phase, practice PYQs and mixed sets that combine reasoning and subject questions. In the final phase, attempt full length mock tests to refine time management.

Mock Tests and Revision

Take timed mock tests regularly and analyze mistakes. Classify errors as comprehension, concept, or careless. Revise weak areas immediately and keep a brief error log for final review.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I choose the optional discipline in XH

Select the discipline that aligns with your academic background and where you can consistently score in practice tests.

Is Section A difficult in XH

Section A is manageable with regular practice in reading comprehension and logical reasoning.

Do I need mathematics for XH

Only basic quantitative reasoning is required in Section A. Most of the paper is concept based.

Are PYQs enough for XH preparation

PYQs are essential, but they should be supplemented with concept revision and targeted practice sets.

Conclusion

The GATE 2026 XH syllabus provides a balanced path for candidates in humanities and social sciences. With consistent reading practice, strong conceptual notes, and regular mock tests, candidates can achieve a competitive score.

Stay aligned with the official syllabus, practice PYQs, and focus on accuracy to make the most of your preparation.
